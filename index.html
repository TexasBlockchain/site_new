Who we are

Texas Blockchain Foundation is a 501(c)(3) non-profit corporation ran by students at The University of Texas, an overseeing advisory board consisting of industry professionals, and corporate members ranging from startups to Fortune 500 companies.

We are dedicated to serving the local Austin blockchain communities by hosting events, incubating startups, and distributing resources. Our members consist of undergraduates, graduates, alumni, professors, and corporate sponsors.

Corporate Members

Education

We teach an open-source blockchain development course each semester, starting from the rise of bitcoin as an alternative currency post Great-Recession, to developing smart contracts, and then ending with a capstone project involving a decentralized-application that each student develops. Along the way, we dedicate lecture time to discuss current happenings in the blockchain space and invite guest speakers to present lectures. As the largest division within Texas Blockchain, the Education department's goal is to serve as a point for all our members to understand the history, politics, and theory behind blockchain, in order to participate in our R&D and Finance departments.

Incubator

In an effort to put Austin on the map as a leader in the blockchain development space, Texas Blockchain Foundation has partnered up with the Cockrell School of Engineering and Blackstone Launchpad to launch a startup-incubator and accompanying Blockchain course for Fall 2018. We believe that hands-on experience with developing products from the ground up in a multidisciplinary and talented team is the recipe to creating the best and brightest blockchain engineers. As part of our incubator, we will offer our members a collaborative environment to develop and release their products, with funding, mentorship and human capital offered through our industry sponsors, board members and students at UT Austin. Among our corporate sponsors are Blackstone, Austin Technology Incubator, ConsenSys and Grid+.
